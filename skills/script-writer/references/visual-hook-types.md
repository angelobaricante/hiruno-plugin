# Visual Hook Types

Point To Visual (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Start with creator on screen and then use finger to point to focus visual

Move In Frame (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Creator walks into or around the frame to give motion to the eye

Camera Whip (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Use fast camera whip motion to create initial stun

Jump In (Subject Motion)  
Starting Point: Creator comes into the screen from the side

Snap/Pop Reveal (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Use some visual signature reveal to appear in the frame

Clone // Body Double (Subject Motion)  
Starting Point: Film yourself twice. (Optional - Animate one of them.) Have both versions of you appear on the screen at the same time

Fly On Wall (Subject Motion) Starting Point: The subject of the video is looking off screen. Common in podcast interview style clips

Anticipated Disaster (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Use clip of a dangerous thing about to happen to subject in video

Object Catch (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Catch item thrown into frame

Using Random Product (Pope In The Pool) (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Use random item or task as a subconscious visual distraction

Setting Down Phone (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Set down recording device as video starts

Holding Prop (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Subject starts by holding/looking at a prop, which becomes focus of POV shot

Many Of Same Prop (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Multiple items. Could be similar or different

Framebreaker (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Remove background from initial visual and overlay it on a normal background so subject appears to break the frame

Jump Switch (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Creator jumps and when they land the scene changes

3P Crash Zoom (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Use a shot of another person in the distance while zooming in

Fridge POV (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Put camera in fridge, cabinet, food aisle, etc, and then remove item/open door to reveal creator

Write On Screen (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Use a marker to write text on camera lens / Animate text

Mirror (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Creator talks to audience from reflection in mirror

Yap Cold Open (Subject Motion) Starting Point: Creator starts talking to audience and there is nothing happening in visual hook

A vs B Comparison Graphics (Graphic/Text Overlays) Starting Point: Use graphics to showcase A vs B of something

Text Slide In (Graphic/Text Overlays) Starting Point: Title text slides into video

Interactive Titles (Graphic/Text Overlays)  
Starting Point: Use the title text to create motion by having it come on the screen in an interesting way

Text Arrow Pointer (Graphic/Text Overlays)  
Starting Point: Use text and an arrow to direct attention at some visual while explaining what it is

Small Image Drop Overlay (Graphic/Text Overlays) Starting Point: Use a small image to drop on a larger image and integrate the small subject into the large

Screen Recording w/ Motion (Graphic/Text Overlays)  
Starting Point: Start with a screen recording using motion to aim the viewer's attention

Image Overlay Motion (Graphic/Text Overlays) Starting Point: Use an image (with or without motion) and text to begin the video

Countdown (Graphic/Text Overlays) Starting Point: Start the video by counting down from 3, 2, 1 before revealing something

High Motion Base B-Roll (Visual Selection) Starting Point: The core base visuals have high motion embedded in them

Unusual First Image/Scene (Visual Selection)  
Starting Point: Use a full screen image of something that visually breaks conventional pattern

Silent Reaction PIP (Visual Selection)  
Starting Point: Creator watches a base clip silently and then reacts

Visual Mistake (Visual Selection)  
Starting Point: Add something unexpected (that often looks like an error or glitch) to the frame

Viral Stitch Reaction (Pattern Interrupt) Starting Point: Start with a viral clip and then react to it as a transition into your video

Match Cut (Pattern Interrupt) Starting Point: Use a rapid series of visuals/text that line up

Visual Switch (Pattern Interrupt)  
Starting Point: Use an action in the first shot to cleanly transition to the second shot

Beat Match Visual Switch (Pattern Interrupt)  
Starting Point: Edit the timing of the clips turning on matched to the beat of the song

Viral Stitch Motion Match (Pattern Interrupt)  
Starting Point: Start with a viral clip and then match cut your own motion to match

Viral Stitch Unlinked Switch (Pattern Interrupt)  
Starting Point: Use a viral clip (high anticipation) and then cold switch to a clip of your brand/business

Speed Ramp Effect (Visual Effect) Starting Point: Add a speed ramp transition in the edit from shot 1 to shot 2

The Zoom In (Visual Effect)  
Starting Point: Zoom in on creator

Look Up At Camera // Top Down (Visual Effect)  
Starting Point: Film using a camera up in the corner of a room to give a different look

Sudden Danger 1P POV (Visual Effect)  
Starting Point: Use clip of a dangerous thing about to happen to viewer and fast switch/stitch

Fish Eye (Visual Effect)  
Starting Point: Film using a fish eye lens to give a different look

Frame Collapse (Visual Effect)  
Starting Point: Slowly collapse the top and bottom of the frame until it reveals an aspect ratio you want, time with beat

Crazy Transition (Visual Effect)  
Starting Point: Using high motion editing transitions or templates in Capcut

Experimental (Interactive) (Visual Effect)  
Starting Point: Use motion graphics on screen to suggest thumb movement, and then edit to match the motion

Color Switch (Visual Effect) Starting Point: Use any visual, start with 0 saturation, change to full color on tap/beat
